<p><p>The prophylactic human vaccine market comprises several types, primarily including inactivated vaccines, attenuated vaccines, and others. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, providing immune response without causing disease; they are often used for diseases like hepatitis. Attenuated vaccines use live but weakened pathogens, offering a strong immune response and longer-lasting protection, such as in the case of measles. Other vaccine types include subunit, conjugate, and mRNA vaccines, which utilize various innovative technologies to enhance immunity and target specific diseases effectively.</p></p>
